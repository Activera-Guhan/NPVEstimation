Basic Details
Analysis Options
Cash Flow Diagram
i
Shows annual net cash flows as a bar chart and cumulative discounted PV as a line. The crossover point of the line at $0 is the project break-even. Bars are green (positive) or red (negative).
Sensitivity Spider Chart
i
Shows how NPV changes as each input variable moves ±20% from its base value. Steeper lines = more sensitive variables. Use Display Variable(s) to filter which lines appear.
Monte Carlo Distribution
i
Runs 10,000 simulated scenarios by randomly varying uncertain inputs. Produces a probability distribution of NPV outcomes and shows P10 (pessimistic), P50 (median), and P90 (optimistic) values.
Text Analysis Summary
i
Produces a plain-language written summary of the model results: base NPV, verdict, P10/P50/P90 range, top risk driver, and confidence level. Useful for client-facing reports.
Input Options
Initial Investment (dollars)
i
The total upfront project budget in dollars ($). This is recorded as a negative cash flow at Year 0. Enter a whole number, e.g. 1000000 for $1M.
Annual Benefit (dollars)
i
The base annual hard benefit this project delivers at full adoption — something that appears directly on a revenue or income statement. Enter dollars per year, e.g. 500000. Soft benefits are added separately below.
Time
i
Duration: total project life in years (e.g. 10). Implementation: years before benefits begin (e.g. 1). Benefits are modelled in the remaining years (Duration − Implementation).
Duration (years)
Implementation (years)
Annual Expenses (dollars)
i
Ongoing annual operating cost once the project is live (opex). Enter dollars per year, e.g. 90000. This is subtracted from benefits each year. Costs inflate over time per the inflation rate.
S-Curve
i
Models the ramp-up in project value over time. Adoption Peak (0–1): fraction of full benefit reached at maturity, e.g. 1.0 = 100%. Ramp Shape: steepness of the logistic curve — higher = sharper ramp, e.g. 2.0 = gradual, 6.0 = steep.

Ramp Shape
Discount, Tax, Inflation, and Overhead Rates
i
All entered as decimals. Discount Rate: required return / WACC, e.g. 0.10 = 10%. Inflation Rate: general price inflation, e.g. 0.03 = 3%. Tax Rate: corporate tax, e.g. 0.25 = 25%. Overhead Rate: indirect cost ratio, e.g. 0.10 = 10%.
